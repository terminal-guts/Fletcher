# Fletcher Frameworks

Systems and tools that operationalize the strategy into actionable research and matching workflows.

---

## Quick Start

**Goal:** Find one genuinely great, underappreciated company and one qualified buyer. Approach the company with the buyer to prove demand.

**Process:**
1. Identify high-signal subsectors (use [AEC Subsector Analysis](./aec-subsector-analysis.md))
2. Research candidate firms from your AIA network (use [Research Workflow](./research-workflow.md))
3. Score each candidate (use [Candidate Scorecard](./candidate-scorecard.md))
4. Identify and qualify buyers from your AIA network (use [Buyer Matching Framework](./buyer-matching-framework.md))
5. Match firms to buyers and make introductions

---

## The Four Frameworks

### 1. [AEC Subsector Analysis](./aec-subsector-analysis.md)
**What it is:** A map of where great + underappreciated firms cluster in the AEC space.

**Use it when:** You're deciding which parts of architecture/engineering/construction to focus on first.

**Key output:** Prioritized list of subsectors with high signal (institutional architecture, engineering specialists, adaptive reuse, commercial/mixed-use).

**For your agents:** This tells them WHERE to look. Start with high-signal subsectors. Ignore low-signal ones.

---

### 2. [Candidate Scorecard](./candidate-scorecard.md)
**What it is:** An evaluation tool that operationalizes the six "great" + six "underappreciated" criteria into a 0-24 point score.

**Use it when:** You've finished researching a firm and need to decide if they pass the bar.

**Key output:** Score (0-24). Pass threshold: 10+ on each dimension, 20+ total.

**For your agents:** This is the decision gate. Research firms until you have enough signal to score them, then rate them on this scorecard. Anything scoring below 20 moves to the next candidate.

---

### 3. [Research Workflow](./research-workflow.md)
**What it is:** A 2.5-hour deep-dive template for researching a single firm candidate.

**Use it when:** You've identified a candidate firm from your network and need to decide if they're genuinely great + underappreciated.

**Key output:** A research note (with portfolio analysis, competitive positioning, gap identification, and the non-obvious observation you'd lead with in an approach).

**For your agents:** This is the playbook. Follow it phase by phase. You'll gather evidence for the scorecard, and you'll find your opening line (the one non-obvious thing you noticed that you'd lead with in a pitch).

---

### 4. [Buyer Matching Framework](./buyer-matching-framework.md)
**What it is:** A system for identifying qualified buyers from your AIA network and matching them to firms.

**Use it when:** You have a firm you want to approach, or you're building a database of potential clients for firms you find.

**Key output:** A list of qualified buyers (people with decision authority, active pipelines, openness to new relationships) matched to specific firms.

**For your agents:** This teaches you who counts as a "qualified buyer" and how to qualify people from the AIA conference. Build a buyer database in parallel with your firm research. When you find a great firm, match them to a buyer. Then approach the firm with proof of demand.

## The System

### Research Track (Parallel)

1. **Subsector Research**
   - Agent: Scan high-signal AEC subsectors (institutional, engineering, adaptive reuse, commercial)
   - Output: Shortlist of 5-10 candidate firms per subsector with brief rationale

2. **Candidate Deep-Dive**
   - Agent: Use the Research Workflow to deep-dive on each candidate
   - Output: Research note (2-3 pages) with scorecard rating

3. **Buyer Database**
   - Agent: Map all AIA conference relationships through the Buyer Matching Framework
   - Output: List of 5-10 qualified buyers with pipeline info, matched to potential firms

### Decision Loop

- **Score ≥20?** Move to buyer matching. Find a buyer for this firm.
- **Score <20?** Move to next candidate.
- **Buyer match found?** Make introduction. Then approach firm with buyer signal.

### Success Condition

✓ One firm scored ≥20 on the scorecard
✓ One qualified buyer matched to that firm
✓ Introduction made; buyer shows interest
✓ Approach ready: "We just connected you with [Buyer]..."

## Using These Frameworks: Checklist for Agents

When you're assigned research on a specific subsector or candidate:

- [ ] **Subsector clarity:** Which subsector am I researching? (Check AEC Analysis to confirm it's high-signal)
- [ ] **Candidate list:** Who are 3-5 candidate firms in this subsector to research?
- [ ] **Deep dive:** For each candidate, follow the Research Workflow (2.5 hours per firm)
- [ ] **Scorecard:** Rate each firm on the Candidate Scorecard; only pass firms scoring ≥20
- [ ] **Buyer matching:** For firms that pass, find matching buyers from the AIA network using the Buyer Framework
- [ ] **Next step:** Present top candidates to Saul with research notes + scores + buyer matches

## Key Definitions

**Great:** Firms that are genuinely excellent at what they do
- Protected by a real moat (craft, relationships, judgment)
- Principals are hands-on practitioners with strong opinions
- High client retention and referral rates
- They've made conviction-driven decisions against industry consensus
- Consistent culture top to bottom
- They say no to work that doesn't fit

**Underappreciated:** Quality has outrun recognition
- Weak web/marketing presence relative to work quality
- Known in one geography or segment, invisible outside
- Referral-dependent but not saturated (capacity available)
- Peer recognition exceeds market recognition
- Pricing doesn't reflect quality
- A recent transition opened a visibility window

**Qualified Buyer:** Decision-maker with active pipeline and openness to new relationships
- Real authority (decides which firms to hire)
- Active projects now or in next 12 months
- Values quality over cost
- Potential for repeat work
- Accessible (warm introduction or open to conversation)
