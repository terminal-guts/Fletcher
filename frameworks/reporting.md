# How to Report Findings

When you finish your research, here's how to present findings back to the hub (Saul/main coordinator).

---

## Three Report Types

### Report Type 1: Subsector Research (Track 1)

**What you did:** Identified high-signal AEC subsectors and shortlisted candidate firms.

**Format:**

```
SUBSECTOR RESEARCH REPORT

Subsector: [Name of subsector]
Signal Score: [18-22 from analysis]
Why High-Signal: [1-2 sentences on why this subsector matters]

CANDIDATE FIRMS (prioritized by confidence):
1. [Firm Name]
   - Why interesting: [1-2 sentences]
   - Quick confidence: [High/Med/Low based on initial signals]
   - Link/contact if known: [How to find them]

2. [Firm Name]
   - Why interesting: [1-2 sentences]
   - Quick confidence: [High/Med/Low]
   - Link/contact if known: [How to find them]

3. [Firm Name]
   - Why interesting: [1-2 sentences]
   - Quick confidence: [High/Med/Low]
   - Link/contact if known: [How to find them]

RECOMMENDATION
Which subsector should Track 2 research deepest first, and why? Rank the top candidates.

[One paragraph with your recommendation and confidence level]
```

**Where to save:** Just report back in a message; don't need a file. But do provide the structure above.

**Timeline:** End of day 1.

---

### Report Type 2: Candidate Deep Research (Track 2)

**What you did:** Deep research on specific firms; scored each on the 0-24 scorecard.

**Format:**

1. **Create a research note file** in `candidates/[Firm-Name].md` using the template from `candidates/TEMPLATE.md`

2. **Report back with a summary message:**

```
CANDIDATE DEEP RESEARCH REPORT

Firms Researched: [Number]

PASSING FIRMS (20+):
1. [Firm Name] — Score: __/24
   - Key observation: [The opening line you'd use in an approach]
   - Gap summary: [1 sentence: what's the mismatch?]
   - Confidence: [High/Med/Low]

[Repeat for any other passing firms]

NEAR MISSES (15-19):
[List any firms that scored well but didn't quite pass, with score and why]

CANDIDATES TO REVISIT:
[Any firms worth deeper research if we have time?]

RECOMMENDATION
Which passing firm should we match to a buyer first, and why?

[1-2 sentences on your top choice and why]
```

3. **Link to the research notes:**
   - "Full research details in `candidates/[Firm-Name].md`"

**Where to save:** 
- Research note goes in `candidates/[Firm-Name].md`
- Summary report as message back

**Timeline:** Day 2-3 after Track 1 prioritizes subsectors.

---

### Report Type 3: Buyer Identification (Track 3)

**What you did:** Mapped AIA relationships and qualified buyers.

**Format:**

1. **Create buyer profile files** in `buyers/[Name]-[Company].md` using the template from `buyers/TEMPLATE.md` for each qualified buyer

2. **Report back with a summary message:**

```
BUYER IDENTIFICATION REPORT

Total AIA Relationships Documented: [Number]

QUALIFIED BUYERS (High potential):
1. [Name] at [Company]
   - Title/Role: [Title]
   - Active Pipeline: [Yes/No/Maybe with timeline]
   - Decision Authority: [High/Med/Low]
   - Ideal firm type: [What kind of firm would excite them?]

2. [Name] at [Company]
   - Title/Role: [Title]
   - Active Pipeline: [Yes/No/Maybe with timeline]
   - Decision Authority: [High/Med/Low]
   - Ideal firm type: [What kind of firm would excite them?]

[Repeat for all qualified buyers]

MEDIUM-POTENTIAL (Worth qualifying further):
[List and brief reason]

NOT QUALIFIED:
[Summary: why certain contacts didn't pass—RFP process, cost-focused, no pipeline, etc.]

RECOMMENDATION
Which 2-3 buyers should we prioritize when we have passing firms to match?

[1 paragraph on your top choices and why they're highest probability conversions]
```

3. **Link to buyer profiles:**
   - "Full profiles in `buyers/[Name]-[Company].md`"

**Where to save:**
- Buyer profile goes in `buyers/[Name]-[Company].md`
- Summary report as message back

**Timeline:** End of day 1 (can update as you find more qualifiers).

## The Synthesis Loop: How Reports Connect

```
Track 1 Report (Day 1)
   ↓ [Hub reads: which subsector to prioritize?]
   ↓
Track 2 Report (Day 2-3)
   ↓ [Hub reads: which firms pass? Score details in candidates/ files]
   ↓
Track 3 Report (Day 1+)
   ↓ [Hub reads: which buyers are qualified? Match to passing firms]
   ↓
HUB SYNTHESIS
   → "Firm X (score: 23) matches with Buyer Y (high authority, active pipeline)"
   → Move to approach phase
```

## Reporting Best Practices

### Be Specific
❌ "This firm seems great"
✓ "This firm has four institutional projects with same university over 8 years; moat is their understanding of that client's complex expansion needs"

### Surface the Doubt
❌ Pretend you're certain
✓ "Score is 21, but I'm medium confidence on the 'unpopular decisions' criterion—need to dig into interviews"

### Highlight the Gap
❌ "Firm is good but not known"
✓ "They've designed 3 award-winning climate resilience projects in last 2 years, but their homepage still reads as 'general structural engineering'—recent transition created a positioning window"

### Link to Evidence
❌ "They have a moat"
✓ "Moat: their structural work on seismic resilience is cited by peer firms but they're one of few specialists in this; can't be replicated quickly. Evidence: [project], [award], [interview quote]"

### State Your Confidence
Don't say "score: 20." Say "score: 20 (HIGH confidence)" or "score: 20 (MEDIUM confidence—need to verify X)"

### Note What You Didn't Check
If you ran out of time to fully vet something, say so. Example: "Didn't find detailed Glassdoor reviews; scored culture as 1 based on limited LinkedIn evidence."

## Quality Checklist Before You Report

Before you submit findings:

**Track 1:**
- [ ] Reviewed AEC Subsector Analysis
- [ ] Identified high-signal subsectors (18+)
- [ ] Found 3-5 candidates per subsector
- [ ] Ranked by conviction/interest
- [ ] Included how to find each firm

**Track 2:**
- [ ] Used 2.5-hour Research Workflow per firm
- [ ] Scored all 12 criteria on Candidate Scorecard
- [ ] Created research note file with full details
- [ ] Identified the one non-obvious gap
- [ ] Stated confidence level clearly

**Track 3:**
- [ ] Documented all known AIA contacts
- [ ] Qualified each on buyer framework
- [ ] Created buyer profile for high-potentials
- [ ] Identified active pipelines and timelines
- [ ] Noted decision authority level

---

## What NOT to Report

- Don't wait for perfection. 80% research confidence = report and move
- Don't include internal deliberation. Report findings, not your thought process
- Don't hedge too much. "This firm seems maybe potentially interesting" = not useful
- Don't mix opinion with evidence. "Great firm" (opinion) vs. "4 repeated clients over 8 years" (evidence)

---

## Questions to Expect

When you report, be ready for:

**Track 1:**
- "Why this subsector over that one?"
- "Who are other candidates we should consider?"
- "Can you find contact info for these firms?"

**Track 2:**
- "Why score 18 and not 20?"
- "What specifically is the gap here?"
- "What would you lead with in an approach?"
- "How confident are you on this observation?"

**Track 3:**
- "Is this buyer really open to new relationships?"
- "What's their actual timeline?"
- "Who else makes the decision?"
- "Would they move fast?"

Have your research notes ready to point to.

---

## The Goal

The hub (Saul/coordinator) needs:
1. **Signal** on which subsectors matter most
2. **Scores** on firms (pass/fail clarity)
3. **Profiles** on buyers (authority, pipeline, openness)
4. **Confidence** levels so we know what to trust
5. **Evidence** (referenced in files, not long messages)

Make the hub's job easy: clear scores, linked details, stated confidence, answered questions before they're asked.
