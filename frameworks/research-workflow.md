# Research Workflow: 2-3 Hour Deep Dive

Template for evaluating a single candidate. The goal: gather enough signal to score them on the scorecard AND find one non-obvious observation to lead with in an approach.

## Phase 1: Portfolio & Public Output (45 min)

Build a complete picture of what they've made and what they say about it.

### 1.1 Portfolio Analysis
- **Website:** Find their projects page. Screenshot or export every project with: location, completion date, client type (public/private), project scale, principal names if listed.
- **Patterns to notice:**
  - Do the same clients appear multiple times? (Retention signal)
  - Are projects getting more complex or simpler over time?
  - Are they staying in one geography or expanding?
  - What's the average project timeline? (Slow delivery vs. fast, scale of work)
  - Are they doing speculative work (competitions, proposals) or only built projects?

### 1.2 Award History
- **Search:** "[Firm name] AIA award", "[Firm name] award", "architecture awards [firm name]"
- **Collect:** Every award listed on their site + others you find.
- **For each award, note:**
  - Award name and year
  - What the award citation says (copy-paste key language)
  - Local (chapter) vs. national?
  - What does the citation reveal about their approach? (Look for words like "innovative," "timeless," "community," "risk," "craft")

### 1.3 Written Content
- **From their website:**
  - Blog posts, articles, white papers, manifestos
  - About/Mission statements
  - Project descriptions (are they detailed and craft-focused, or marketing copy?)
- **External:**
  - Search "[Principal name]" + "article", "essay", "published"
  - Check: LinkedIn, Medium, Medium publications, trade journals, design publications
- **What you're looking for:**
  - How do they talk about their work? Precise or vague?
  - What do they believe about craft, clients, the industry?
  - Any evidence of contrarian thinking?

**Time checkpoint:** 45 min elapsed. You should have: complete project list, award history, and sample of their written voice.

---

## Phase 2: Third-Party & Market Signals (45 min)

What do others say about them? What's the market context?

### 2.1 Press & Media Mentions
- **Search:** "[Firm name]" on Google News, regional design publications (Architect Magazine, local AIA chapters, construction journals)
- **What to collect:**
  - Who writes about them and in what context?
  - Do they proactively pitch stories or only get covered when projects complete?
  - Tone of coverage: how are they described?

### 2.2 Peer Recognition
- **AIA Directory:** If they're a member, what's listed? (Often includes brief bios/focus areas)
- **Design/Trade Publications:** Do other architects cite them? Are they mentioned in "rising firms" or "best of" lists?
- **LinkedIn:** How many architects follow them? Do peer firms comment on their posts?
- **Search:** "[Firm name] known for", "[Firm name] reputation", "[Principal name] respected for"

### 2.3 Market Competitive Set
- **Find their competitors:** Who are they usually mentioned alongside? Search "[Firm name] vs", "[Firm name] similar to", or "[Firm name] competitor"
- **Compare:** Pick 2-3 peer firms at similar scale. Compare their web presence, award history, social media activity, pricing positioning.
- **The gap:** Is this firm less visible than peers despite similar or better work?

### 2.4 Client Base & Segment
- **From portfolio:** What types of clients do they serve? (Developers, institutions, government, corporations)
- **Breadth:** Do they have one anchor client or diverse base?
- **Market size:** Are they serving a niche (e.g., only religious institutions) or broad market?

**Time checkpoint:** 90 min elapsed. You should have: press coverage summary, peer recognition map, competitive positioning, client segment clarity.

---

## Phase 3: Depth Signals & the Gap (45 min)

Find the inconsistency between quality and visibility. This is where you find your opening line.

### 3.1 Culture & Team
- **Glassdoor/Indeed:** Read recent reviews (last 2 years). What do employees say about the firm? Culture, work, compensation, principals?
- **LinkedIn:** Check principal profiles. How long have key team members been there? Are people leaving or staying?
- **Hiring:** Are they actively hiring? What do job postings say about the firm culture/values?
- **What you're looking for:** Is the culture as consistent as the strategy claims? Do staff seem proud to work there?

### 3.2 Pricing & Economics
- **Direct:** If you can find fee schedules or hear from an industry connection, what are they charging?
- **Comparative:** Are they cheaper or more expensive than peers? By how much?
- **Signal:** Do they compete on price or differentiation?
- **Growth:** Are they growing revenue faster than peers or slower? (Slow growth + capacity available = potential underappreciation)

### 3.3 The Gap: Quality vs. Recognition
- **Synthesis:** Do you see a clear mismatch? Are they doing work that should command national attention but remaining locally known?
- **What caused it?**
  - Weak marketing/web presence
  - Recent leadership change not yet reflected in market perception
  - Niche focus that limits broader visibility
  - Humility/lack of self-promotion
  - Undercapitalized marketing efforts
  - New service line not yet known
- **Is it sustainable?** Would they be happy to stay small, or is there untapped demand?

### 3.4 The Non-Obvious Observation
- **Write one sentence** that you'd open a pitch with. Not: "You do good work." But: something you noticed that most researchers wouldn't dig for.
- **Examples:**
  - "You've taken on increasingly complex institutional work over the past five years, but your positioning still leads with residential—seems like an earned opportunity gap."
  - "We noticed you turn over projects at [pace] vs. peer firms at [pace], which suggests you're either underpriced or doing something more thoughtful per project."
  - "Every client you've worked with more than once is in the [sector], but your website barely mentions it—seems like the real focus area is hidden."
  - "Your award history is all local/regional; your peer set has gone national. The work looks equally strong; the visibility gap is something else."

**Time checkpoint:** 135 min elapsed. You have the gap and opening line.

---

## Phase 4: Scoring & Decision (15 min)

Use the [Candidate Scorecard](./candidate-scorecard.md) template.

### 4.1 Rate All 12 Criteria
- Work through the scorecard
- Use evidence from your research above

### 4.2 Note Disqualifying Factors
- Does anything rule them out?

### 4.3 Final Call
- **Pass threshold:** 10/12 on each dimension, 20+ total
- **Next step:** Score ≥ 20? Move to outreach prep. Below? Move to next candidate.

## Time Breakdown (Ideal)

- Phase 1 (Portfolio): 45 min
- Phase 2 (Third-party): 45 min
- Phase 3 (Depth): 45 min
- Phase 4 (Scoring): 15 min
- **Total:** ~2.5 hours

If you find a strong candidate early, you might do a deeper dive (closer to 3+ hours). If you're eliminating quickly, move to the next candidate.

---

## Research Tools

- **Portfolio mining:** Website screenshot tools (Evernote Web Clipper, Notion Web Clipper), Google Images (for project searches)
- **Award history:** AIA.org search, regional AIA chapter websites, award publication archives
- **Media:** Google News, design publication sites (Architect Magazine, Architizer, Design Observer)
- **Team/Culture:** LinkedIn, Glassdoor, Indeed
- **Competitive:** Similar searches for peer firms using the same approach
- **Contact/intel:** LinkedIn connection requests to people at the firm, AIA directory, conference attendee lists
