## Company 1: Stratus Engineering, Inc.

**Location:** Houston, Texas  
**Founded:** 1996  
**Founder/Leadership:** Don Schneider, P.E. (Founder, President)  
**Estimated Headcount:** 15-25 (private firm, not public data)

### Craft Moat
- **35+ years of founder expertise** in process design, troubleshooting, and process safety across crude units, vacuum units, and specialty processes
- **Deep simulation experience** across multiple modeling platforms; process troubleshooting through fundamental thermodynamics and separation science, not just pattern matching
- **Litigation support and expert witness work** creates additional credibility and recurring client relationships
- **Founder-led problem solving:** Don Schneider remains actively involved in technical work, not management-only

### Great Signals
1. **Persistent client relationships:** Multiple refinery clients with decades-long engagement; repeat troubleshooting contracts suggest stickiness and trust
2. **Technical publications and thought leadership:** Articles in Oil & Gas Journal on specialized topics (e.g., alkylation unit DIB analysis) suggest deep expertise and industry recognition
3. **Process safety management expertise:** PSM and process hazard analysis (PHA) work for refinereries suggests clients rely on them for critical safety infrastructure
4. **40+ year track record in petroleum industry:** Founder's background before 1996 suggests even deeper legacy

### Underappreciated Signals
1. **Minimal public web presence:** Website is functional but dated; no blog, no case studies, no marketing presence; compete entirely on referral
2. **No major awards or recognition circuit:** Industry awards are absent; unknown to broader petrochemical conference circuit
3. **Not on industry analyst lists:** Large EPC firms dominate media; Stratus invisible to casual market research
4. **Capacity-constrained:** Founded in 1996 and still <25 people suggests deliberate smallness; likely turning away work and only taking ideal clients
5. **No online portfolio or project showcase:** Clients discovered through industry networks, not Google or industry databases

### Non-Obvious Observation
Stratus appears to be a "trust tier" firm: too specialized and boutique to advertise, so they only get client calls from insiders who already know their reputation. They've survived and thrived for 30 years on pure technical moat and referrals—no marketing, no scale ambitions. This is typical of process engineering boutiques where the founder's technical reputation IS the company.

## Company 2: Petro Project Engineering, Inc.

**Location:** Houston, Texas (moved from New Orleans in 1990)  
**Founded:** 1980  
**Founder/Leadership:** R. D. Kulkarni, P.E. (Founder)  
**Estimated Headcount:** 20-30 (private firm)

### Craft Moat
- **46-year tenure** across crude unit and vacuum unit process design; 100+ assignments across U.S., UK, China, Iraq
- **Process simulation specialization:** Mastery of multiple simulators (Aspen Plus, HYSYS, PRO II, Design II/Winsim); founder chose Design II early and built unmatched expertise there
- **Front-End Engineering Design (FEEDS) specialists:** Rare skillset; core business is advanced process design simulation before capital expenditure—exactly what refiners need for revamps
- **Founder-driven technical judgment:** R.D. Kulkarni, a licensed professional engineer, personally leads designs; founder hasn't retreated to management-only role

### Great Signals
1. **100+ assignments since 1980 for major refiners and chemical producers:** Track record across ExxonMobil, Valero, Chevron equivalents (inferred from client list breadth)
2. **International scope and credibility:** Work in Iraq Kurdistan, West Africa, and UK suggests deep industry relationships and ability to navigate complex projects
3. **Early adoption of simulation technology:** First job (1980) used Simsci's PROCESS for crude distillation; company has been at forefront of simulation methodology for 46 years
4. **Sustainability across three decades:** Moved office from New Orleans to Houston in 1990; weathered multiple oil price cycles and still operating

### Underappreciated Signals
1. **Extremely weak web presence:** Website is barebones (http://petroproject.com) with minimal detail; no social media, no blog, no thought leadership
2. **No public thought leadership or publications:** Not visible in trade press; no articles in Digital Refining, Hydrocarbon Processing, or Oil & Gas Journal that would reveal their work
3. **Founder anonymity in market:** R.D. Kulkarni is unknown outside tight process engineering circles; zero LinkedIn visibility, no industry speaking engagements
4. **FEEDS business invisible to broader market:** Process design boutiques are the least visible segment in EPC; they work pre-capital, before major EPC firms take over
5. **No awards or certifications:** Not on any "best engineering firms" lists; no industry association leadership visible

### Non-Obvious Observation
Petro Project Engineering is a "process simulation first" firm in a market where simulation is now commoditized in software. But their stickiness comes from founder judgment—knowing which design creates the best economicsfor a specific refinery, which simulation tool to use for what problem, and how to translate simulation into operational reality. They've survived 46 years because their clients (refinery design engineers, revamp project managers) keep coming back for that judgment. The founder-led model is key: Kulkarni still owns and runs the firm.

## Company 3: Furnace Improvements Services (FIS)

**Location:** Sugar Land, Texas (Houston area, Ship Channel proximity)  
**Founded:** 1996  
**Founder/Leadership:** Ashutosh G. (Founder)  
**Estimated Headcount:** 20-40 (operates U.S. and India offices)

### Craft Moat
- **Fired heater specialization:** 29+ years of deep expertise in the single most critical and failure-prone piece of refinery equipment (furnaces/fired heaters)
- **Proprietary heater design methods:** Patented technologies for heater revamps, CFD modeling, and performance optimization; not available through generic engineering
- **Multi-disciplinary expertise:** Covers combustion engineering, mechanical design, materials, burner systems, controls—a rare combination for boutique firm
- **Founder-led technical team:** Ashutosh G. remains active in technical design and troubleshooting; not a sales-only leadership model

### Great Signals
1. **29-year operating history without acquisition or major capital raising:** Independence and organic growth suggest sustainable business model and sticky client base
2. **Global reach:** Work across U.S., Canada, and international clients; India operations suggest capability to serve global refineries
3. **Patented technologies:** IP in fired heater design is rare; suggests technical innovations competitors can't replicate
4. **Training and consulting services:** Courses for engineers and operators create additional revenue and deepen client relationships
5. **CFD expertise:** Computational fluid dynamics for furnace design puts them in rare technical tier

### Underappreciated Signals
1. **Minimal web marketing:** Website is functional but sparse; very few case studies or project examples visible
2. **Not on refinery consulting lists:** When refiners search "furnace expert," major EPC firms (Becht, Lummus) dominate; FIS is unknown to non-specialists
3. **No major industry awards or certifications:** Not visible in API or refining association leadership
4. **Underground reputation:** Known only by refinery operations and engineering teams who've had furnace problems; marketing invisible to decision-makers unfamiliar with furnace-specific work
5. **No thought leadership presence:** No articles in trade press; no speaking engagements at refining conferences visible

### Non-Obvious Observation
FIS is a "furnace emergency hotline" firm: refiners don't know about them until a fired heater fails or underperforms. Once they solve the problem (often through field troubleshooting + detailed engineering), refiners keep them on retainer for future issues. Patented designs create switching costs. The founder remained technical (rather than sales-driven) which is rare for a 30-year-old company—suggests true engineering culture and prioritization of craft over growth.

## Honorable Mention (Acquired/Changed Model)

### Houston Integrity Consultants (HIC) — Now Part of PROtect LLC

**Location:** Houston, Texas  
**Founded:** 2012 (Founded by John Tischuk, acquired March 2023)  
**Founder/Leadership:** John Tischuk (late), now led by Stuart Beveridge  
**Status:** Acquired by PROtect LLC (March 2023)

**Why it no longer fits the pattern:** HIC was founded by John Tischuk, a 40+ year industry veteran who pioneered Risk-Based Inspection (RBI) and helped write API RP 580 (the standard for RBI methodology). From 2012-2023, it was a pure boutique. However, the 2023 acquisition by PROtect LLC removed the founder-led, independent status. Now part of a larger mechanical integrity roll-up. Still worth noting as an example of how these firms become acquisition targets once they prove their model.
