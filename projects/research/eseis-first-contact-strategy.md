# eSeis, Inc. — First-Contact Strategy

**Company:** eSeis, Inc.
**Location:** Houston, Texas
**Founded:** 1995
**Focus:** Seismic Petrophysics for oil & gas exploration, drilling, and production
**Contact:** Roger Young, CTO & Founder (pioneer of LithSeis, QX, DrilSeis)

**Date Evaluated:** June 14, 2026
**Evaluator:** Codene Research Team

---

## Executive Summary

eSeis is a genuinely great, systematically underappreciated technical leader that has pioneered an entire discipline (seismic petrophysics) but remains nearly invisible outside the independent oil & gas operator world. They have an ironclad moat (proprietary, invented-here technologies + 30 years of industry expertise), practitioners at the helm (Roger Young invented their core tools), and deep client retention within a specific segment. The gap: they serve 9,000+ independent operators and exploration-stage companies explicitly excluded by Schlumberger, CGG, and Paradigm—but they're invisible to their own target market. No web presence worth mentioning. No marketing. Known only by referral.

**The bet:** Connect them with a tier of independent operators and exploration firms that don't know eSeis exists but desperately need their tools. Unlock a pipeline of customers that the major software vendors have written off.

## Part 3: Customer Delivery Entry Strategy

### What Customer Type Unlocks eSeis's Growth?

**Target Segment:** Independent E&P operators and exploration firms with active drilling/exploration budgets who lack access to premium seismic analysis tools.

**Profile:**
- 50-500 person companies
- Acquisition budgets: $10M-$100M+ per year
- Decision-makers: VP Exploration, VP Drilling, Chief Geoscientist
- Current workflow: Limited seismic tools, use public data, hire expensive one-off consultants
- Pain point: Can't afford Petrel. Don't have in-house seismic expertise. Miss opportunities because they can't interpret their own seismic properly.
- Willingness to pay: High (seismic tools justify ~$50K-$500K per year to avoid drilling dry holes)

**Market Size:**
- ~9,000 independent E&P operators in US alone
- ~3,000+ exploration firms actively seeking new ventures
- eSeis has penetrated ~120 of 9,000 = 1.3% of US market
- Penetration in global independent market: <1%

### Value Transformation: Unlocking This Pipeline

**What gets unlocked:** 
- Access to a customer segment that is desperately underserved by the major software vendors
- A repeatable customer acquisition engine (currently broken; now functional)
- Potential to capture 5-10% of independent operator market within 3 years = additional $20M+ in recurring revenue at scale

**How to measure success:**
1. **Near-term (6 months):** 5-10 qualified introductions to independent operators with active exploration programs
2. **Medium-term (12 months):** 3-5 new customer contracts with eSeis from the pipeline
3. **Long-term (24 months):** Establish eSeis as the go-to tools provider for independent operators (brand + visibility)

## Part 4: How You Actually Unlock This Pipeline

### Step 1: Identify & Qualify Independent Operators (6-8 weeks)

**Research Deep:**
- Build a database of independent E&P operators actively drilling in US (Texas, Oklahoma, Louisiana, Wyoming, New Mexico)
- Segment by: Active exploration programs + recent deals/capital raises + decision-maker accessibility
- Target ~150 qualified operators across 3-4 high-activity geographies

**Data sources:**
- IHS Markit (company financials, project tracking)
- Wood Mackenzie (upstream capital spend data)
- Regional drilling permits (TRRC, OCC, COGCC—public records)
- LinkedIn (VP Exploration, Drilling contacts)
- Deal databases (PitchBook, Crunchbase for fundraises)

**Qualification criteria:**
- Active drilling in past 12 months
- Exploration-stage or recent capital raise (signals active budgets)
- VP Exploration or similar decision-maker identifiable
- No existing relationship with major software vendor (Petrel, CGG)

### Step 2: Problem Research Conversations (8-10 weeks)

**Conduct 20-30 exploratory calls with VP Exploration / Chief Geoscientists:**
- "We're studying how independent operators make drilling decisions. How do you currently interpret seismic data?"
- Listen for: current tools, pain points (cost, capability gaps, time to interpret), frequency of dry holes
- Don't pitch eSeis; gather intelligence on the workflow and the bottleneck

**Output:** 
- Validated pain profile (cost vs. capability tradeoff)
- List of 10-15 "high-fit" operators (clear seismic workflow gaps, budget, decision authority)

### Step 3: Make Strategic Introductions (8 weeks)

**For each high-fit operator, execute the introduction in this order:**

1. **Approach eSeis first** with evidence of demand:
   > "We've spent the past 8 weeks talking to 25 independent E&P operators. There's a clear gap: they need seismic petrophysics analysis but can't afford or access Petrel. Every operator we've talked to has this problem. We want to introduce you to 5 of the most qualified operators on our list who are actively exploring."

   - Attach: brief profiles of the 5 operators (name, budget, pain point, decision-maker)
   - Ask: "Are you open to exploring these introductions?"
   - Signal conviction: "We're doing this because we see an obvious gap in how the market serves independents."

2. **Approach the operator** with eSeis context:
   > "I've been studying how independent operators make drilling decisions. I came across eSeis—they pioneered seismic petrophysics specifically for operators like you, because they know Petrel isn't an option. They've built tools that work for exploration-stage companies. I want to introduce you."

   - Attach: one-pager on LithSeis/QX (what it does, why it matters for exploration risk)
   - Ask: "Are you open to learning more about a seismic analysis tool built for operators?"

3. **Make the introduction:**
   > "I'm introducing you both. [Operator], [eSeis contact], you should talk. [Operator] is actively exploring in the Permian and needs seismic analysis. [eSeis], you built tools for exactly this use case."

   - Keep it brief. Let them talk.

### Step 4: Support the Win (Ongoing)

Once a conversation starts:
- Sit in calls to understand eSeis's pitch + operator's blockers
- Help eSeis understand the operator's budget and timeline
- Help the operator understand eSeis's technology (translate technical to business impact)
- Drive toward a pilot project or contracted analysis work (not just conversation)

**Success metric:** First customer contract from the pipeline within 6 months.

Subject: Independent operators looking for seismic analysis tools (not Petrel)

Hi Roger,

I've been studying how independent E&P operators make drilling decisions. Over the past few weeks, I've talked with geoscientists at 25 independent operators across Texas and Oklahoma.

One pattern keeps coming up: they need better seismic analysis, but they can't justify Petrel's cost or complexity. They end up hiring expensive one-off consultants or missing opportunities because they lack the tools.

When I looked at what's built specifically for that segment, eSeis stood out. LithSeis and QX are the tools they actually need.

The problem: most of these operators have never heard of you. They're looking, but not finding.

We're currently connecting high-growth independent operators with the tools and partners they need. I'd like to explore whether we could help eSeis reach 5-10 of the most qualified operators we've identified who are actively exploring and have budget for seismic analysis.

Are you open to having a conversation about this?

Thanks,
Saul

---

**Why this works:**
1. **Leads with problem research, not pitch** (shows we understand the gap)
2. **Positions us as bringing demand to them** (not asking for help; offering value first)
3. **Specific ask** (5-10 introductions, not vague "partnership")
4. **No sell** (we're not pitching; we're asking if they want to talk)
5. **Signals conviction** (we've done 8+ weeks of research before calling)

## Part 6: Alternative Friction Points (If Customer Delivery Doesn't Resonate)

If the independent operator pipeline doesn't excite Roger or Adam, here are three other leverage points:

### 1. **Technology Modernization & Cloud Migration**
**Friction:** eSeis's core tools (LithSeis, QX, DrilSeis) are proprietary but desktop-based. Moving to cloud + collaborative interfaces would unlock remote consulting and SaaS pricing.

**Angle:** "We can help you modernize the tech stack to enable cloud-based analysis and remote collaboration. This unlocks a subscription pricing model and makes the tools accessible from anywhere (exploration firms globally)."

**Why it matters:** Current model is service + software hybrid. Cloud SaaS model could 3x pricing power and reduce customer acquisition cost (self-serve onboarding).

### 2. **Thought Leadership & Industry Positioning**
**Friction:** Roger Young is a legend inside the industry but unknown outside. Seismic petrophysics is not a known discipline to most oil & gas executives or investors.

**Angle:** "You pioneered an entire discipline, but nobody outside the oil patch knows it. We can help you build thought leadership—publications, speaking, a visible vision for the future of seismic petrophysics. Position eSeis as the canonical thought leader."

**Why it matters:** Elevates brand, attracts better talent, enables premium pricing, creates barriers to new competitors in their segment.

### 3. **Strategic Partnerships with Drilling Service Providers**
**Friction:** Drilling service companies (SLB Services, NOV, etc.) deal with the same independent operators. They have direct relationships with VP Drilling. eSeis does not.

**Angle:** "We can help you build distribution through drilling service partnerships. They refer eSeis to their operator clients as part of a bundled drilling optimization package."

**Why it matters:** Leverages existing relationships, makes eSeis a default tool in drilling workflows, reduces customer acquisition cost, increases switching costs.
