# Sourcing Tracker: Measuring What Works

Track where candidates come from and which channels produce the best companies.

---

## How to Use

**For each sourced candidate, document:**

1. **Company name**
2. **Sourcing channel:** Which of the 5 channels (below)?
3. **Date sourced**
4. **Initial assessment:** Does it seem promising? (Yes/Maybe/No)
5. **Status after evaluation:** Pass/Fail on gates

**Sheet location:** `sourcing-tracker.csv` (below)

---

## The 5 Sourcing Channels

From Stage 0.1 of the methodology (in priority order):

1. **Industry Research Agent** — Dedicated research producing candidate pipeline
2. **Personal Network & Relationships** — Saul's connections, referrals from investors/advisors
3. **Industry Conferences & Events** — Companies/founders met at events (e.g., AIA conference)
4. **Public Data Signals** — Growth patterns, hiring, news (hiring surge but stalled revenue, etc.)
5. **Customer/Vendor Networks** — Introductions via people who work with the company

## Measuring Effectiveness

**Calculate for each channel (monthly/quarterly):**

| Metric | How to Calculate | Target |
|--------|------------------|--------|
| **Hit Rate** | (Companies that PASS evaluation) / (Companies sourced) | 20-30% |
| **Time to Research** | Average hours spent per candidate | <10 hours |
| **Cost per Candidate** | (Time invested) × (hourly rate) | <$[TBD] |
| **Effort to Source** | Hours per week per channel | Track for ROI |

## Why Track This?

1. **Learn what works** — Some channels produce better candidates
2. **Allocate effort efficiently** — Know where to spend research time
3. **Identify patterns** — "Tech companies from this network always have X block" = repeatable opportunity
4. **Feedback loop** — Update methodology sourcing strategy based on actual data

---

## When to Review

- **Monthly:** Quick scan of candidates sourced and pass rates
- **Quarterly:** Deep analysis by channel; recommend priority shifts
- **Before methodology update:** Reference sourcing effectiveness data

## Notes for Agents

- **Document as you source.** Don't wait until quarter-end.
- **Be honest about effort.** Time tracking helps us learn.
- **Flag interesting signals.** If you notice a pattern ("all oil & gas companies seem to have sales blocks"), note it for manager review.
- **Questions?** Ask manager before rejecting a channel as "not working"—sometimes the channel is fine, we just need more data.
