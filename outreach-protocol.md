# Outreach Protocol: Follow-Up Cadence & Decision Rules

Stage 2 guidance: How many touches before deciding to move on?

---

## The Approach

From Stage 2.1 of methodology, in order of preference:

1. **Warm introduction** (Highest success rate)
2. **Direct outreach from Saul** (More credible than third-party)
3. **Team member outreach** (If specialized fit)

---

## Follow-Up Sequence

### Path 1: Warm Introduction (via Mutual Connection)

**Week 1:** Mutual connection sends intro email
- Introduce Saul to founder
- Brief context: "He helps great companies stuck on [blocking factor]"
- No immediate response expected

**Week 2:** Saul follows up directly (if no response from intro)
- Reference the introduction
- Send the observation of their blocking factor
- Ask for 15 min call

**Decision point (Week 3):**
- **If founder responds:** Schedule call. Proceed to Stage 3 (Value Proposition)
- **If no response after 5-7 days:** Send ONE follow-up email (low-pressure)
- **If still no response:** Move to next candidate

### Path 2: Direct Outreach from Saul

**Week 1:** Send initial email
- Subject: [Observation of blocking factor]
- Body: What you noticed → The gap → Small ask (read brief?)
- One email, no urgency

**Week 2:** If no response, send ONE follow-up (5-7 days after first email)
- Light touch: "Wanted to check if the brief landed"
- Link to original observation
- "No pressure if timing isn't right"

**Decision point (Week 2-3):**
- **If founder responds:** Schedule call. Proceed to Stage 3
- **If no response after follow-up:** Move to next candidate

### Path 3: Team Member Outreach

Same cadence as Path 2, but:
- First contact from specialist (e.g., regulatory expert on permitting issues)
- If no response after 1 outreach + 1 follow-up: Escalate to Saul for direct outreach or move on

## Email Templates

### Initial Outreach Email

```
Subject: [Observation] — [Specific, non-obvious gap you noticed]

Hi [Name],

[HOOK] When [source], we kept hearing about [specific work they do well].

[GAP] Looking at [evidence], it seems like [their gap] — quality / recognition / positioning / growth hasn't caught up.

[ASK] We put together some thoughts on what we think could change this. Worth 10 minutes to see if we've understood the situation correctly?

[SIGN-OFF] [Name]
```

### Follow-Up Email (if no response)

```
Subject: Re: [Original subject]

Hi [Name],

Just wanted to check if the brief landed — no pressure if timing isn't right.

Happy to chat whenever works.

[Name]
```

## When to STOP Pursuing a Company

Move to next candidate if:

1. **No response after initial outreach + 1 follow-up** (typically 2 weeks)
2. **Founder declines or says "not interested"** (respect it; they know their situation)
3. **Founder says they're in crisis mode** (fundraising, lawsuit, major transition)
4. **Founder doesn't recognize the blocking factor** (They're not aware of their own problem = low chance of fixing it)
5. **Blocking factor seems unsolvable in 3-6 months** (Revisit in 12 months)

---

## When to CONTINUE Despite No Quick Response

**Hold a company as "warm" (don't give up immediately) if:**

- Founder is busy but gave positive signal ("interesting, but timing is off now")
- Company just went through major transition (new hire, funding, office move) → revisit in 6 weeks
- Seasonal business (might be mid-busy period)
- You have strong conviction from research (gate 3 analysis is really solid)

**For "warm" companies:**
- Follow up quarterly
- Reference previous outreach
- Update on new observation if available
- "Thinking of you for a different reason now..."

---

## Response Scenarios

### Founder Says YES to a Call

→ **Stage 3: Value Proposition pitch**
- 15-30 min conversation
- Understand their situation deeper
- Clarify what you offer
- If still interested: Set up deeper discovery call

### Founder Says "Interesting but Wrong Timing"

→ **Keep warm**
- Ask: "When would be good timing?" (if they offer a timeframe, respect it)
- Add to quarterly follow-up list
- Don't push

### Founder Says "Not Interested / Not a Good Fit"

→ **Respect the decision**
- Thank them for their time
- "If situation changes, we're here"
- Document their reasoning for future reference
- Move on

### Founder Doesn't Respond After Follow-Up

→ **Move to next candidate**
- Document attempt (source, dates, observation)
- Note for manager: "Pattern emerging?" (if multiple companies in same sector don't respond)
- Batch these into quarterly outreach review

## Success Metrics (Quarterly Review)

- **Response rate:** (Positive responses) / (Outreach attempts) — Target: 25-35%
- **Time to response:** Average days from outreach to response
- **Path effectiveness:** Which approach path (warm intro vs. direct) works better?
- **Conversion rate:** (Companies that move to Stage 3) / (Positive responses) — Target: 80%+

---

## Manager Notes

- **This protocol is provisional.** Once we have real outreach data, manager will refine based on what actually works.
- **If you hit edge cases** (founder is ghosting, company signals interest then disappears), flag to manager.
- **Timing matters.** If founder says "call me in 3 months," honor it. Don't bug them monthly.
- **Respect their decision.** If they say no, that's data. Don't hard-sell.
