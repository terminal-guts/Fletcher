# Evaluation Checklist: The Three Gates

Quick reference for evaluating companies against the methodology. Print or bookmark this.

---

## Gate 1: Greatness Screening

### Financial Quality (must meet 2+ of 3)

| Dimension | Threshold | How to Find |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| Profitability | ROE > 12% OR ROIC > WACC + 2% | Annual report / SEC filings / Ask founder |
| Leverage | Net Debt / EBITDA < 2.5x OR Debt / Assets < 0.4 | Balance sheet (public) or estimate from news |
| Earnings Quality | Operating CF > 80% of Net Income | Cash flow statement or proxy: "revenue is real" signal |

**For private companies (no public data):**
- Ask founder directly: "Are you profitable? By how much?"
- Ask: "Do you have debt? How much?"
- Proxy signal: "Are you growing revenue and retaining employees?" = likely profitable
- If data truly unavailable: proceed to moat assessment; don't reject on missing data alone

**Decision:** 2+ criteria met → proceed to moat. Fewer than 2 → REJECT

---

### Competitive Moat (must score 2+ as "Strong")

Score each 0-2: **0 = None/Weak, 1 = Moderate, 2 = Strong**

**1. Network Effects** — Does value increase with scale?
- Q: Do more users = more valuable for each user?
- Examples: Marketplace, platform, multi-user software
- Strong if: switching cost is high due to network

**2. Intangible Assets** — Brand, reputation, patents, relationships
- Q: Would customers specifically choose this company over a copycat?
- Examples: Trusted brand, long-term vendor relationships, proprietary process
- Strong if: Hard to replicate trust; customers prefer this company

**3. Cost Advantage** — Can they operate cheaper than competitors?
- Q: Do they have a structural cost edge, not just good management?
- Examples: Proprietary tech, lean ops, scale, supplier relationships
- Strong if: Competitors can't match without major investment

**4. Switching Costs** — How hard/expensive is it for customers to leave?
- Q: What would a customer lose by switching?
- Examples: Deep integration, custom workflows, training investment
- Strong if: Customer would lose significant $$$ by switching

**5. Scale** — Does size itself create advantage?
- Q: Do larger players have structural advantages here?
- Examples: Economies of scale in purchasing, production, distribution
- Strong if: Smaller competitors can't compete on price/reach

**Decision:** 2+ moats scored as "Strong" → proceed to Gate 2. Fewer than 2 → REJECT

## Gate 2: Blocking Factor Identification

### Step 1: Identify the Blocking Factor

Find ONE specific reason this great company isn't winning as much as it should.

**Categories to explore:**

| Category | Examples | How to Find |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| **Sales/Market Access** | Poor sales process, weak marketing, distribution problem, underpriced | LinkedIn (job postings?), website (do they market?), interviews, growth trajectory |
| **Operations** | Manual workflows, tech debt, poor financial controls, execution inconsistency | Team size vs. revenue, employee tenure, what they say about their process |
| **Regulatory** | Permitting burden, legal exposure, market entry barriers | News, interviews, industry knowledge |
| **Technology** | Technical debt, product-market fit gaps, missing capability | Product age, team composition, customer feedback |
| **Market/Timing** | Market awareness (invisible), network disadvantage, category definition | Search visibility, industry knowledge, peer citations |

**Non-blocking factors (REJECT if this is the "block"):**
- "Need capital" (that's funding, not a solvable block)
- "Market timing is bad" (we can't fix timing)
- "Founder doesn't want to grow" (doesn't fit our model)

---

### Step 2: Validate the Blocking Factor

Ask these 4 questions. All must be YES to proceed.

| Question | What It Means | Red Flags |
|----------|---------------|-----------|
| **Is it specific and repeatable?** | Not vague ("we need to grow") but concrete ("we can't scale sales without a CRM and process"). The founder recognizes it. | "We just need more money" or "market timing isn't right" |
| **Is it solvable in 3-6 months?** | Removing the block unlocks growth within 3-6 months. Not a 5-year culture shift or fundamental product rebuild. | "We need to completely redesign our product" or "we need a different founder" |
| **Would solving it unlock growth?** | Direct path to better financial outcomes. Not a nice-to-have, but a bottleneck. | Solving it results in +5% revenue; ignoring it doesn't hurt |
| **Is it NOT their core competency?** | They're already great at X; this block is in Y. They don't need to outsource their strength. | Founder says "we're actually really good at sales; we just chose to focus on product" |

**Decision:** All 4 YES → proceed to Gate 3. Any NO → REJECT

## Gate 3: Addressability & Opportunity Sizing

### Step 1: Can We Help?

Does the blocking factor map to something Saul's team can address?

| Blocking Factor | Can We Help? | How |
|---|---|---|
| Sales process / lead gen | **YES** | Build systems, playbooks, training |
| Marketing / brand | **MAYBE** | Positioning help (limited scope) |
| Regulatory / permitting | **YES** | On-ground work, relationships, process |
| Operations / processes | **YES** | Workflow design, automation, training |
| Tech debt / product | **YES** | Engineering help, modernization |
| Market access / network | **MAYBE** | Introductions, relationship building |
| Pricing strategy | **MAYBE** | Analysis + positioning help |
| Fundamental product gap | **NO** | They need internal team, not external help |

**Decision:** Must map to YES or MAYBE (with clear hypothesis). If NO → REJECT

---

### Step 2: Is the Opportunity Worth It?

Rough estimate:

- **Current Revenue:** [estimate if needed]
- **3-Year Revenue if block solved:** [based on founder input or peer benchmarks]
- **Revenue Uplift:** (3-year revenue) - (current revenue)
- **Implementation Timeline:** Typically 3-6 months for external help
- **Team Effort:** How many people, how many weeks?

**Quick filter:** 
- Revenue uplift < $1M/year? Consider rejecting (low impact)
- Implementation > 6 months and 3+ full-time people required? Consider rejecting (high effort)

**Decision:** If ROI looks positive (impact > effort cost) → proceed. Otherwise → REJECT

---

### Step 3: Will This Work for Multiple Companies?

Can the same solution help others?

- **High repeatability:** Sales system problem (helps 5+ similar companies) ✅
- **Medium repeatability:** Regulatory burden in specific state (helps others in same jurisdiction)
- **Low repeatability:** One-off tech rebuild (bespoke to this company)

**Note:** High repeatability is nice but not required. A high-impact one-off is worth pursuing.

---

## PASS EVALUATION

If company passes all three gates → Ready for outreach (Stage 2)

## Decision Tree (Quick Reference)

```
Company Sourced
    ↓
Gate 1: GREATNESS?
  ├─ Financial (2+ of 3)?  NO → REJECT
  ├─ Moats (2+ strong)?    NO → REJECT
  └─ YES → Gate 2

Gate 2: BLOCKING FACTOR?
  ├─ Specific & repeatable?        NO → REJECT
  ├─ Solvable in 3-6 months?       NO → REJECT
  ├─ Unlocks growth?               NO → REJECT
  ├─ Not their core competency?    NO → REJECT
  └─ YES → Gate 3

Gate 3: ADDRESSABILITY?
  ├─ Maps to our capabilities?     NO → REJECT
  ├─ Revenue uplift > $1M/year?    NO → CONSIDER REJECT
  ├─ Timeline < 6 months?          NO → CONSIDER REJECT
  └─ YES → ✅ PASS EVALUATION

✅ PASS → Ready for Outreach
```

## When Stuck

| Situation | What to Do |
|-----------|-----------|
| Can't find financial data | Ask founder directly. If unavailable, note it and proceed to moat assessment. Don't reject on missing data. |
| Founder doesn't recognize the blocking factor | Not a good fit. Great companies that don't see their own block won't fix it. REJECT. |
| Blocking factor seems both critical AND unsolvable in 3-6 months | REJECT. Revisit in 12 months if situation changes. |
| Hard to assess moat (private company, niche market) | Look for: Do customers come back? Do competitors exist in this space? Do they charge premium prices? |
| Company seems great but no obvious blocking factor | They might not be a fit. "Already winning" companies don't need us. REJECT or HOLD for later. |
